Congratulations to all! The Transition Team is extremely pleased that so many people are willing to volunteer to help make Metafilter better, and that 828 members participated in MetaFilter's first vote. We, and no doubt the rest of the community, look forward to seeing where they will guide us!

The next step for the Steering Committee is setting up their communication methods, determining specific consensus process(es), and chatting with the staff and jessamyn. They'll likely have their own MetaTalk announcing all that in the next couple of weeks.

That’s it, thanks again everyone!

Forgive my confused asking please, were we allowed to vote for seven or twelve self-nominees, or some other number entirely? I (mis?)understood seven, and that seven would serve.

Folks had seven votes for twelve sitting members, with the Transition Team appointing the remaining 5. (This was implemented ad hoc based on Jpfed's very helpful commentary on balancing diversity and representation in earlier Metatalks; you can see a lot of the reasoning going back and forth in and around the MeTa comment I've linked here. Jpfed has since volunteered as a contributor to help with implementing voting for future cycles, which is a great resource available to the new Steering Council.) The Transition Team made our five choices after the votes were in, with an eye towards maintaining diversity on the board in case the popular vote didn't effectively do that.

As it happened, the Transition Team's unanimous choices were... essentially identical to the popular vote tallies for the remaining five spots, so it wound up being a little redundant this round! That's okay, though; redundancy exists in order to provide backup solutions in case things are weird and unpredictable, and I felt pretty good about how well the general line-ups matched.
