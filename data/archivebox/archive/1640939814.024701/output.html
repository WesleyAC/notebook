Synecdoche, New York

2008 Directed by Charlie Kaufman

Synopsis

A theater director struggles with his work, and the women in his life, as he attempts to create a life-size replica of New York inside a warehouse as part of his new play.

  • Review by thatothermitch ★★★★½

    This review may contain spoilers. I can handle the truth.

    A gifted but troubled man's obsessive attempts to capture the meaning of life prevents him from creating meaning in his own. His grandiose, failed visions of broad, deliberate strokes contrast with his first wife's highly successful attempts to capture meaning on the micro scale. He understands by the end that there is no audience waiting to receive his message, but the impossible pursuit itself becomes the only possible meaning left.

    Fascinating, sickening, hilarious, rich and heartbreaking. Deserving of a rewatch.
